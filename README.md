## 1. Career Road‑Map

| Phase | What to Do | Typical Time |
|-------|------------|--------------|
| **High‑School / Early College** | • Start with programming (Python, C++)<br>• Take Algebra, Calculus, Statistics, and Intro to Computer Science<br>• Build small projects (IoT, games) | 0‑2 yrs |
| **Undergraduate (BSc/BA)** | • Major in Computer Science, Electrical Engineering, Data Science, Mathematics, or Physics<br>• Electives: Machine Learning, Deep Learning, AI Ethics, Robotics, NLP<br>• Join clubs: AI/ML hackathon, robotics, data‑science meet‑ups | 4 yrs |
| **Graduate / Certifications** | • MSc/PhD in AI, ML, Data Science, or CS<br>• Professional certs: Google Cloud AI/ML Engineer, NVIDIA Deep Learning, AWS Certified Machine Learning – Specialty | 1‑3 yrs |
| **Early‑Career (Intern/Junior Engineer)** | • Internships at AI labs, start‑ups, or industry R&D comps<br>• Build portfolio projects, Kaggle competitions, open‑source contributions | 0‑3 yrs |
| **Mid‑Career (Senior/Lead AI Engineer)** | • Lead AI product or R&D teams<br>• Design AI strategy, manage large scale ML pipelines, build production‑grade models | 3‑7 yrs |
| **E‑x‑Advanced** | • AI Research Scientist/Principal Engineer<br>• Publish papers, speak at conferences | 7+ yrs |

**Formatting Tips**

- **Font**: Calibri or Arial, 10‑12 pt.
- **Margins**: 1" on all sides.
- **Bullet style**: Use circles or squares.
- **Section Headers**: Bold and slightly larger (e.g., 12–13 pt).
- **Consistency**: Use the same tense (past for past roles, present for current).
- **Length**: 1 page for <5 yrs experience; 2 pages otherwise.

## 5. Additional Tips

| Topic | Tip |
|-------|-----|
| **Cover Letter** | Personalize for each role; highlight 1–2 projects that align with the company’s mission. |
| **Interview Prep** | Practice coding on whiteboard (Python, SQL, algorithmic problem solving). Prepare to explain model choices, trade‑offs, and deployment details. |       
| **Portfolio** | Host notebooks, dashboards, and demos on **GitHub** and a personal website (GitHub Pages, GitLab Pages, or Netlify). |
| **Networking** | Join LinkedIn groups like “AI & Machine Learning”, attend industry meet‑ups, and actively engage on Twitter/Reddit. |
| **Music** | Keep learning! Read latest papers in *arXiv* domain sections (cs.AI, cs.LG). |
| **Soft Skills** | Work on communication: present research to non‑technical audiences. |

---

### Quick Checklist

1. **Educational Foundation** – BSc + relevant coursework + at least one ML/AI specialization.
2. **Core Technical Skills** – Python, ML libraries, data pipelines, CI/CD.
3. **Certifications** – Google Cloud, AWS, NVIDIA (vital for cloud‑based AI roles).
4. **Hands‑on Projects** – 2–3 end‑to‑end AI products, open‑source contributions.
5. **Internships** – Target AI labs or R&D teams.
6. **Resume** – Use the template above, tailor for each company.
7. **Portfolio** – Online demos, Kaggle kernels, GitHub repo.
8. **Networking** – Conferences, meet‑ups, LinkedIn.

Happy building, and good luck on your journey to becoming an AI Engineer!
